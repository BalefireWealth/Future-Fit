Industry Outlook · The Structural Flaw

A model that requires markets to cooperate.

We noticed three structural features of the PE aggregator model that function correctly in exactly one market environment. We don't say this as a criticism—leverage and shorter hold periods aren't inherently the wrong choice. But the math only really works in one direction.

Structural Feature 01
AUM-Based Revenue
Management fees tied to assets under management. When markets stagnate, revenue compresses—without any corresponding reduction in fixed operating costs or debt obligations.
Structural Feature 02
8× Leveraged Debt
Acquisition financing at 8× EBITDA must be serviced regardless of market conditions. Purchased at 9×–15× EBITDA multiples. There is no pause button on debt service during a consolidation cycle.
Structural Feature 03
5–7 Year Exit Windows
PE firms purchased at 9×–15× EBITDA multiples and require favorable conditions to exit. A consolidation in the exit window means holding an impaired asset at a cost of capital that does not forgive.
